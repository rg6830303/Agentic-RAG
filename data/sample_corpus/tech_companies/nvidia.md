# NVIDIA Corporation

NVIDIA Corporation is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. It was founded on April 5, 1993, by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. Jensen Huang has served as the chief executive officer (CEO) and president of NVIDIA since its founding.

## Business segments
- **Graphics**: GeForce GPUs for gaming, RTX series with real-time ray tracing.
- **Data center**: H100, H200, GB200, and Blackwell-generation accelerators used to train large language models such as GPT, Llama, and Claude.
- **Automotive**: NVIDIA DRIVE platform for autonomous vehicles.
- **Professional visualization**: Quadro/RTX workstation GPUs used in design, simulation, and media production.

## CUDA and the AI platform
CUDA, introduced in 2006, is NVIDIA's parallel computing platform and API model that allows developers to use NVIDIA GPUs for general-purpose computing. CUDA is the dominant software stack for training and serving deep neural networks today, and is a primary moat for NVIDIA's data center business.

## Market position
By 2024, NVIDIA's data-center segment had become the largest revenue contributor, driven by demand for AI training and inference compute. NVIDIA briefly became the most valuable publicly traded company in the world in 2024, surpassing Microsoft and Apple by market capitalization.
