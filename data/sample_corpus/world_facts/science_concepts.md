# Foundational Science Concepts

## Physics
- **Newton's three laws of motion** describe inertia, F = ma, and action–reaction pairs.
- **Einstein's special relativity (1905)** introduced time dilation, length contraction, and E = mc² for objects moving at velocities approaching the speed of light.
- **General relativity (1915)** describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass-energy.
- **Quantum mechanics** governs subatomic behavior; key principles include superposition, entanglement, the uncertainty principle, and wave–particle duality.
- **Thermodynamics** has four laws (zeroth through third), covering thermal equilibrium, energy conservation, entropy, and absolute zero.

## Chemistry
- **Periodic table** organizes elements by atomic number, with groups sharing valence-electron configuration and similar chemistry.
- **Chemical bonds**: ionic (electron transfer), covalent (electron sharing), metallic (delocalized electrons), hydrogen bonds (weak intermolecular).
- **Acids and bases**: Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, and Lewis definitions; pH measures hydrogen ion concentration on a log scale.
- **Redox reactions** involve electron transfer; oxidation is loss of electrons, reduction is gain.

## Biology
- **Cell theory**: all living organisms are composed of cells; the cell is the basic unit of life.
- **DNA** is the double-helix molecule that stores genetic information, structure solved by Watson, Crick, Franklin, and Wilkins in 1953.
- **Evolution by natural selection**, proposed by Charles Darwin in *On the Origin of Species* (1859), explains the diversity of life through heritable variation and differential reproductive success.
- **CRISPR-Cas9** is a precise gene-editing tool adapted from a bacterial immune system; Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier shared the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for its development.

## Astronomy
- **The observable universe** is roughly 93 billion light-years in diameter; its age is about 13.8 billion years.
- **The Milky Way** is a barred spiral galaxy containing 100–400 billion stars.
- **Exoplanets**: over 5,500 confirmed as of recent counts, many discovered by Kepler and TESS missions.
- **Black holes** are regions of spacetime with gravitational pull strong enough that nothing — not even light — can escape past the event horizon.

## Mathematics
- **Calculus**, developed independently by Newton and Leibniz in the late 17th century, formalizes rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation (integrals).
- **Linear algebra** studies vector spaces and linear maps; foundational for graphics, ML, and physics.
- **Probability and statistics** quantify uncertainty; the central limit theorem and the law of large numbers underpin much of modern data science.
