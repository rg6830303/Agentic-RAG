# Notable Companies and Markets

## Magnificent Seven (US tech leaders by market cap)
1. **Apple Inc.** — founded 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, Ronald Wayne. CEO: Tim Cook. Products: iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple Watch, Vision Pro, Services.
2. **Microsoft Corporation** — founded 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen. CEO: Satya Nadella. Cloud (Azure), Windows, Office, GitHub, LinkedIn, OpenAI partnership.
3. **Alphabet Inc.** (Google) — Google founded 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. CEO of Alphabet and Google: Sundar Pichai. Search, Android, YouTube, Cloud, Gemini AI.
4. **Amazon.com, Inc.** — founded 1994 by Jeff Bezos. CEO: Andy Jassy. E-commerce, AWS cloud, Prime, devices.
5. **Meta Platforms, Inc.** — founded 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg (and others) as Facebook; renamed Meta in 2021. CEO: Mark Zuckerberg. Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Reality Labs.
6. **NVIDIA Corporation** — see tech_companies/nvidia.md. CEO: Jensen Huang.
7. **Tesla, Inc.** — founded 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning; Elon Musk joined as Chairman in 2004 and later became CEO. Electric vehicles, energy storage, autonomous driving.

## Major non-US tech firms
- **Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC)** — world's largest contract chip foundry. CEO: C.C. Wei. Manufactures chips for Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm, and most leading semiconductor designers.
- **Samsung Electronics** — South Korean conglomerate, leading memory chip maker and smartphone vendor.
- **ASML** — Dutch company, sole supplier of EUV lithography systems used to manufacture the most advanced chips.
- **Tencent** — Chinese tech giant, owns WeChat, major game publisher.
- **Alibaba Group** — Chinese e-commerce and cloud leader.

## Stock market basics
- The **S&P 500** tracks 500 large US companies. The **NASDAQ Composite** is heavily tech-weighted. The **Dow Jones Industrial Average** tracks 30 major US firms.
- Outside the US: **FTSE 100** (UK), **Nikkei 225** (Japan), **DAX** (Germany), **CAC 40** (France), **Hang Seng** (Hong Kong), **Nifty 50** and **Sensex** (India), **Shanghai Composite** (China).
- **ETFs** (exchange-traded funds) trade like stocks but hold baskets of assets — popular examples: SPY (S&P 500), QQQ (Nasdaq-100), VTI (total US market), VWO (emerging markets).

## Recent business trends (2022–2025)
- The 2023 banking stress led to the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank, Signature Bank, and First Republic; the FDIC backstopped uninsured deposits.
- AI infrastructure spending surged, with hyperscalers (Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta) committing over $200 billion combined in 2024 capital expenditures.
- The 2024 US presidential election cycle reshaped tariff and tech policy expectations.
- Crypto markets saw renewed institutional interest after the SEC approved spot Bitcoin and Ether ETFs in 2024.
